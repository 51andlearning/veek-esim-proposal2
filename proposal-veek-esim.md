## 3. The opportunity

### The market
- ~10–11 million Brazilians travel internationally each year (pre-pandemic baseline, fully recovered by 2025).
- The global travel eSIM market is on track for **~USD $4.4B by 2030**.
- Average international roaming spend per Brazilian traveller is reported at $30–$80 per trip; travel eSIMs typically cut that by 60–80% while delivering better speed.
- Brazilians are among the most mobile-app-dependent travellers in the world (banking OTPs, ride-hail, translation, maps) — meaning the cost of being offline abroad is unusually high.

### Why Veek wins
- **Trust:** A Veek customer already trusts the Veek app for their primary line. The leap to trusting it for a travel line is small.
- **Distribution:** The Veek app and the Gigacoins economy are an owned channel — zero CAC for incremental sales.
- **Brand fit:** "The only operator that offers FREE internet" extends naturally into "no bill shock when you travel."
- **MVNE-ready:** The MVNE partner handles the connectivity plumbing, GSMA-compliant eSIM provisioning, and global wholesale. Veek owns the customer, the brand, and the margin.

## 5. The Veek proposition

A Veek-branded, itinerary-aware, app-native travel eSIM with Gigacoins built in.

### Three pillars

**Itinerary-aware.** The Veek app suggests the right plan based on the destination and trip length. If a customer is in Lisbon and adds a side-trip to Madrid, the app offers a regional Europe plan, not two single-country plans.

**Gigacoins-integrated.**
- **Earn:** Buying a travel eSIM credits Gigacoins to the customer's balance.
- **Burn:** Customers can pay for travel data partially or wholly in Gigacoins — closing a new loop in the loyalty economy and giving Premium/Ultra customers a reason to hold a balance.

**100% Veek brand.** End-to-end in the Veek app and on `veek.com.br`. The MVNE partner is invisible to the customer.

### Customer journey

1. **Trigger** — Customer books a flight (or appears in a travel-flagged segment); Veek surfaces a personalised eSIM tile.
2. **Choose** — Bundle is pre-selected for the destination. Customer can edit GB / duration / country group.
3. **Pay** — Card, Pix, or Gigacoins (or any mix).
4. **Install** — One-tap eSIM install via Veek app (eUICC profile push).
5. **Travel** — Auto-activate on first attach abroad. In-app usage meter.
6. **Top up / extend** — One tap, in-app.
7. **Return** — Veek's home plan resumes seamlessly. Trip summary, Gigacoins earned, prompt to rate.

## 7. Why these numbers are defensible
- The 10–20% peak penetration is **annual**, not lifetime, against the Veek active base. Globally, travel-eSIM penetration among MVNO bases that distribute through their own app trends from 2–4% in year one to 8–15% by year three when promoted across all touchpoints.
- The 5GB / $18.07 bundle reference matches mid-range regional plans in the open travel-eSIM market — competitive with Airalo, Holafly, and Saily on a per-GB basis.
- Base growth at +5% YoY is conservative against Veek's recent acquisition trajectory.
- Gigacoins integration is expected to **lift repeat-purchase rates by 15–25%** (illustrative — to be measured in market).

## 8. Integration approach

### What sits where
- **Veek owns:** the customer relationship, the app UX, pricing, brand, marketing, customer support tier 1, the Gigacoins ledger.
- **MVNE partner owns:** eSIM provisioning, global wholesale connectivity, eUICC profile management, network selection, fraud monitoring, customer support tier 2 escalation, regulatory compliance with destination networks.
- **Shared:** the data model (purchase, activation, usage, top-up events flow in both directions via a documented API).

### Build phases
1. **Discovery (4 weeks)** — Validate customer base, ARPU assumptions, Gigacoins economics, app integration surface area, ANATEL/LGPD posture.
2. **MVP (8–10 weeks)** — Single bundle (5GB / 7 days / global), purchase + activation + usage meter in the Veek app, BRL-only payment.
3. **Expansion (next 12 weeks)** — Multi-bundle catalogue, regional plans, Gigacoins earn-and-burn, push-notification journeys, top-up.
4. **Optimise (ongoing)** — Itinerary-aware recommendations, predictive offers, cross-sell into adjacent products.
